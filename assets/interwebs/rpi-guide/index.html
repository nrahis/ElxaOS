Raspberry Pi 4 Quick Start Guide

A Beginner's Guide to Getting Started

What You'll Need

  • Raspberry Pi 4 Model B
  • Power supply (USB-C, 5V/3A)
  • microSD card (16GB+ recommended)
  • HDMI cable (micro-HDMI to HDMI)
  • USB keyboard and mouse
  • Display/monitor

Basic Specifications

CPU: 1.8GHz quad-core ARM
RAM: 1GB/2GB/4GB/8GB LPDDR4
GPU: VideoCore VI
USB Ports: 2x USB 2.0, 2x USB 3.0
Network: Gigabit Ethernet, Wi-Fi 5
Bluetooth: 5.0, BLE

Getting Started

  1. Download the Raspberry Pi Imager from raspberrypi.org
  2. Insert your microSD card into your computer
  3. Open Raspberry Pi Imager and choose your OS (Raspberry Pi OS recommended for beginners)
  4. Select your SD card and click 'Write'
  5. Insert the microSD card into your Raspberry Pi
  6. Connect your peripherals (keyboard, mouse, display)
  7. Plug in the power supply to boot
Note: For first-time boot, make sure you're connected to a display to complete initial setup!

Basic Commands

# Update your system sudo apt update sudo apt upgrade # Check CPU temperature vcgencmd measure_temp # Check system info uname -a # Shutdown safely sudo shutdown -h now

Troubleshooting

No Power LED? Check your power supply and USB-C connection
No Display? Ensure HDMI cables are firmly connected
Won't Boot? Try re-imaging your SD card
Overheating? Ensure proper ventilation or add a heatsink
