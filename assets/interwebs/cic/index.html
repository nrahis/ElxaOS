Introduction to Chihuahuas

The Chihuahua (Canis lupus familiaris var. chihuahuensis) represents one of the smallest breeds of domestic dog. Named after the Mexican state of Chihuahua, these diminutive canines have been the subject of extensive research in veterinary medicine and canine behavior studies.

Anatomical Characteristics

Key Measurements:
  • Height: 6-9 inches (15-23 cm)
  • Weight: 2-6 pounds (0.9-2.7 kg)
  • Skull Circumference: 10-14 inches (25.4-35.6 cm)

Historical Background

Archaeological evidence suggests that the Chihuahua's ancestors were present in ancient Mesoamerican civilizations, particularly among the Toltec civilization where small dogs known as "Techichi" were kept as companions and served ceremonial purposes.
