About the Museum
Where Discovery Meets Wonder

The Snake Valley National Museum has been Snakesia's premier natural history institution for nearly 80 years. Founded in 1947 by Dr. Cornelius Fang, the museum houses over 12,000 specimens and artifacts spanning the geological, biological, and cultural heritage of our great nation.

From the ancient reptiles that once roamed the Snakesian highlands to the dazzling mineral formations found deep beneath the Cobraline Mountains, our collections tell the story of Snakesia's extraordinary natural world. Whether you're a lifelong learner or a curious kid, there's something here for everyone.

Permanent Exhibits
Five Halls of Discovery
Hall of Ancient Reptiles
Step back 200 million years to walk among the prehistoric creatures of ancient Snakesia. Featuring the complete skeleton of a Snakesian Titanosaur and over 400 fossil specimens.
Geological Wonders
Marvel at glittering gemstones, towering crystal formations, and volcanic rock from the Cobraline Mountains. Includes the famous 340-carat Snakesian Star emerald.
Living World Gallery
Life-sized dioramas showcasing Snakesia's diverse ecosystems — from the misty highland forests to the sun-drenched coastal wetlands. Over 60 species represented.
Cultural Heritage Wing
Trace the history of Snakesian civilization through artifacts, tools, textiles, and art spanning thousands of years. Features a reconstructed early Snakesian settlement.
Discovery Lab
Our interactive hands-on science center! Dig for fossils, examine specimens under microscopes, build earthquake-resistant structures, and more. Perfect for young explorers and families.
